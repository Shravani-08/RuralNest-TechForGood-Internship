Health Awareness

Evidence-based guidance for healthy pregnancy and motherhood.

First Trimester (0–12 weeks)

  • Register for antenatal care early
  • Take folic acid daily (400 mcg)
  • Avoid alcohol, tobacco, raw foods
  • Get blood tests and first ultrasound
  • Rest and manage morning sickness

Second Trimester (13–26 weeks)

  • Anatomy scan at 18–20 weeks
  • Iron & calcium supplements important
  • Mild exercise like walking is safe
  • Monitor fetal movements from 20 weeks
  • Dental check-up recommended

Third Trimester (27–40 weeks)

  • Weekly check-ups near due date
  • Birth plan discussion with midwife
  • Watch for preeclampsia signs
  • Hospital bag ready by 36 weeks
  • Breastfeeding preparation

Postnatal Period

  • Mother's postnatal check at 6 weeks
  • Baby's heel prick test within 5 days
  • Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months
  • Watch for postpartum depression signs
  • Family planning counselling

Key Nutrients

  • Folic acid – prevents neural tube defects
  • Iron – prevents anaemia (dal, spinach, meat)
  • Calcium – bone development (milk, ragi)
  • Protein – baby's growth (eggs, lentils)
  • Vitamin D – sunlight + supplements

What to Eat

Eat 5 small meals a day. Include green leafy vegetables, seasonal fruits, whole grains (jowar, bajra), dairy, eggs, and pulses. Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily. Jaggery is a good iron source.

What to Avoid

  • Raw or undercooked meat/fish
  • Unpasteurised milk/cheese
  • Excess caffeine (>200 mg/day)
  • Alcohol completely
  • Street food with poor hygiene

Weight Gain Guide

Normal BMI: gain 11–16 kg total. Underweight: 12–18 kg. Overweight: 7–11 kg. Your RuralNest worker will monitor this at each visit.

First Week

  • Breastfeed within 1 hour of birth
  • Keep baby warm (kangaroo care)
  • Cord care: keep dry and clean
  • Eye drops and Vitamin K injection
  • BCG and Polio vaccination at birth

Danger Signs in Newborn

  • Not feeding at all
  • Fast breathing (>60 breaths/min)
  • Yellow skin (jaundice) spreading
  • Fever or hypothermia
  • Convulsions

Immunisation Schedule

  • Birth: BCG, OPV, Hep B
  • 6 weeks: DPT, OPV, Hib
  • 10 weeks: Booster
  • 14 weeks: Booster + IPV
  • 9 months: Measles, Vitamin A

Breastfeeding Tips

Feed on demand, 8–12 times per day. Ensure deep latch. Use both breasts each feed. No water needed for 6 months. Colostrum (first milk) is golden — never discard it.

Hand Hygiene

Wash hands with soap for 20 seconds before eating, after toilet, before handling baby, and after coming in from outside. This prevents 40% of neonatal infections.

Safe Water & Sanitation

  • Boil or filter drinking water
  • Use clean vessels for storage
  • Wash fruits and vegetables well
  • Avoid open defecation
  • Use proper toilet facilities

Birth Hygiene

  • Deliver at health facility
  • Trained attendant at birth
  • Sterile cord cutting
  • No harmful cord applications
  • Clean delivery surface

Menstrual Hygiene

  • Change pads/cloth every 4–6 hours
  • Wash reusable cloths with soap
  • Dry in sunlight to kill bacteria
  • Dispose sanitary waste properly
